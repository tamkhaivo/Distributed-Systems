## ðŸ§  Core Philosophy (The "Battle-Hardened" Axioms)

1.  **Everything Fails, Always**: If your design assumes the network is reliable, you have already failed. Design for partition, latency, and chaos.
2.  **"Exactly-Once" is a Myth**: You get "at-least-once" and idempotency, or "at-most-once" and data loss. Choose your poison. Use unique keys and check them.
3.  **Clock Skew is Real**: Never trust a timestamp from a client. Spanner has TrueTime; you probably just have NTP and a prayer. Logical clocks (Lamport, Vector) > Wall clocks.
4.  **Consistency is a Spectrum**: Strong consistency is expensive. Eventual consistency is complex to reason about. Know the cost of your read/write paths.
5.  **Simplicity > Cleverness**: A complex consensus algorithm you don't fully understand will wake you up on call. Raft is understandable; Paxos is for when you want to feel smart (and suffer).
